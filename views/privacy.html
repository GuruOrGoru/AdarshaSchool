Privacy Policy

Your privacy and data protection are our top priorities

Our Commitment to Privacy

Adarsha Secondary School is committed to protecting the privacy and security of all personal information we collect and process

At Adarsha Secondary School, we understand the importance of privacy and are committed to protecting the personal information of our students, parents, staff, and visitors. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website or use our educational services.

Last Updated:

This Privacy Policy was last updated on January 1, 2024. We may update this policy from time to time, and any changes will be posted on this page with a new effective date.

Information We Collect

We collect various types of information to provide educational services and maintain school operations

Student Information

We collect personal information about our students, including:

  • Basic identification (full name, date of birth, gender, student ID)
  • Contact information (home address, phone numbers, email)
  • Emergency contact details (parents/guardians, relatives)
  • Academic records (grades, transcripts, attendance records)
  • Medical information (health conditions, allergies, medications)
  • Photographs and videos for school activities and yearbook
  • Disciplinary records and behavioral assessments
  • Special educational needs and accommodations

Parent/Guardian Information

We collect information about parents and guardians, including:

  • Names, contact details, and relationship to student
  • Employment information and emergency contacts
  • Financial information for fee payments and financial aid
  • Communication preferences and language preferences
  • Volunteer activities and involvement in school events

Staff Information

We collect information about our employees and staff, including:

  • Personal identification and contact information
  • Educational qualifications and certifications
  • Employment history and performance records
  • Background check results and references
  • Emergency contact information
  • Banking information for payroll purposes

Website and Digital Information

When you visit our website or use our digital services, we may collect:

  • IP address, device information, and browser type
  • Pages visited, time spent on our site, and navigation patterns
  • Referring website information and search terms
  • Cookies and similar tracking technologies
  • Form submissions and online applications
  • Communication logs and correspondence

How We Use Your Information

We use collected information to provide educational services and improve our school operations

Educational Services

  • Providing academic instruction, assessment, and support
  • Maintaining comprehensive student records and transcripts
  • Tracking academic progress and performance
  • Developing individualized education plans (IEPs) when needed
  • Conducting parent-teacher conferences and meetings
  • Organizing educational field trips and extracurricular activities
  • Managing special education services and accommodations
  • Preparing students for higher education and career readiness

Student Safety and Well-being

  • Maintaining a safe and secure school environment
  • Managing emergency situations and medical emergencies
  • Monitoring student attendance and truancy
  • Addressing behavioral issues and disciplinary matters
  • Providing counseling and mental health support
  • Conducting background checks for visitors and volunteers
  • Implementing anti-bullying and harassment prevention programs

Administrative Operations

  • Processing admissions applications and enrollment
  • Managing tuition payments and financial aid programs
  • Maintaining staff employment records and payroll
  • Complying with educational regulations and reporting requirements
  • Managing school facilities and transportation services
  • Conducting school-wide assessments and evaluations
  • Maintaining alumni records and communications

Communication and Engagement

  • Sending important school announcements and updates
  • Sharing academic progress reports and report cards
  • Providing information about school events and activities
  • Responding to parent and student inquiries
  • Publishing school newsletters and publications
  • Managing social media and online presence
  • Facilitating parent-teacher association communications

Legal and Regulatory Compliance

  • Complying with federal and state education laws
  • Reporting to educational authorities and government agencies
  • Maintaining records as required by law
  • Responding to legal requests and court orders
  • Protecting against fraud and ensuring data integrity
  • Conducting internal audits and compliance reviews

Information Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell personal information and only share it in specific circumstances

Educational Authorities and Government Agencies

We may share information with:

  • Ministry of Education: For compliance with educational regulations and reporting requirements
  • District Education Office: For student enrollment verification and academic reporting
  • Examination Boards: For student registration and examination purposes
  • Higher Education Institutions: For student transcripts and recommendation letters
  • Government Agencies: As required by law or for statistical purposes

Service Providers and Vendors

We may share information with trusted third parties who assist in school operations:

  • Technology Providers: For student information systems and educational software
  • Transportation Services: For school bus and transportation management
  • Food Service Providers: For meal planning and dietary accommodations
  • Security Services: For campus security and safety monitoring
  • Financial Institutions: For fee processing and financial aid management
  • Healthcare Providers: For student health services and medical emergencies

Emergency Situations

We may share information in emergency situations, including:

  • Medical Emergencies: With healthcare providers and emergency services
  • Safety Emergencies: With law enforcement and emergency responders
  • Child Protection: With child welfare agencies when required by law
  • Natural Disasters: With emergency management agencies

Legal Requirements

We may share information when required by:

  • Court Orders: When legally compelled to do so
  • Subpoenas: When served with valid legal process
  • Law Enforcement: When investigating criminal activities
  • Regulatory Compliance: When required by educational or government regulations

With Parental Consent

We may share information with third parties when:

  • Explicit Consent: Written permission is provided by parents/guardians
  • Educational Partners: For collaborative educational programs
  • Scholarship Organizations: For student scholarship applications
  • Research Institutions: For educational research (with appropriate safeguards)

Important Note:

We never sell, rent, or trade personal information to third parties for marketing purposes. All information sharing is done in accordance with applicable laws, regulations, and our commitment to protecting student privacy. We require all third parties to maintain appropriate security measures and use the information only for the purposes for which it was shared.

Data Security and Protection

We implement appropriate security measures to protect your personal information

Technical Security Measures

  • Data Encryption: All sensitive data is encrypted both in transit and at rest using industry-standard encryption protocols
  • Secure Infrastructure: Our servers and databases are protected with multiple layers of security controls
  • Access Controls: Role-based access controls ensure only authorized personnel can access specific information
  • Network Security: Firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and regular security monitoring
  • Regular Updates: All systems are regularly updated with the latest security patches
  • Backup and Recovery: Regular backups with secure storage and tested recovery procedures

Administrative Security Measures

  • Staff Training: Regular training on data protection practices and privacy policies
  • Background Checks: All staff undergo thorough background checks before employment
  • Confidentiality Agreements: All staff sign confidentiality agreements regarding student information
  • Incident Response: Established procedures for responding to data breaches or security incidents
  • Regular Audits: Periodic security audits and compliance reviews
  • Physical Security: Secure storage for paper records and restricted access to school facilities

Data Retention and Disposal

We follow strict data retention policies:

  • Student Records: Retained for a minimum of 7 years after graduation or withdrawal
  • Academic Transcripts: Permanently retained for official purposes
  • Financial Records: Retained for 7 years as required by law
  • Staff Records: Retained according to employment law requirements
  • Secure Disposal: When data is no longer needed, it is securely destroyed using certified methods
  • Legal Requirements: Some records may be retained longer if required by law or regulation

Student and Parent Rights

Students and parents have the following rights regarding personal information:

  • Access Rights: Request access to personal information we maintain about you or your child
  • Correction Rights: Request corrections to inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Deletion Rights: Request deletion of personal information (subject to legal and educational requirements)
  • Portability Rights: Request a copy of personal information in a portable format
  • Consent Withdrawal: Withdraw consent for certain data processing activities
  • Complaint Rights: File complaints with relevant data protection authorities
  • Opt-out Rights: Opt out of certain communications and data uses

Data Breach Response

In the event of a data breach, we will:

  • Immediately investigate and contain the breach
  • Assess the scope and impact of the breach
  • Notify affected individuals and authorities as required by law
  • Implement additional security measures to prevent future breaches
  • Provide support and resources to affected individuals
  • Conduct a thorough review and update of security procedures

Cookies and Tracking Technologies

How we use cookies and similar technologies on our website

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device when you visit our website. They help us provide you with a better experience and understand how our website is being used.

Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential Cookies: Necessary for the website to function properly
  • Analytics Cookies: Help us understand how visitors use our website
  • Preference Cookies: Remember your settings and preferences
  • Marketing Cookies: Used to deliver relevant content and advertisements

Managing Cookies

You can control cookies through your browser settings. However, disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website. For more information about managing cookies, please visit your browser's help section.

Children's Privacy Protection

Special protections for students under 18 years of age

Special Protections for Minors

We recognize that students under 18 years of age require special privacy protections. We have implemented additional safeguards for student data, including:

  • Enhanced parental consent requirements for data collection
  • Restricted data sharing with third parties
  • Limited use of student data for marketing purposes
  • Special procedures for handling student photos and videos
  • Age-appropriate privacy notices and communications

Parental Rights and Controls

Parents and guardians have the right to:

  • Review and request copies of their child's educational records
  • Request corrections to inaccurate information
  • Opt out of certain data collection activities
  • Request deletion of their child's information (subject to legal requirements)
  • Receive notifications about data breaches affecting their child

Important Note:

We never knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 without verifiable parental consent. If you believe we have collected information from a child under 13 without proper consent, please contact us immediately.
